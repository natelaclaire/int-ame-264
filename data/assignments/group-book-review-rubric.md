# Integrated Rubric: Group Book Review Project

**Total: 100 Points**

---

## A. Written Review (60 points)

### 1. Understanding of the Book (15 pts)

* Clearly explains the book’s focus, argument, and scope
* Demonstrates accurate understanding
* Avoids excessive summary

**Excellent (13–15):** Clear, precise, insightful explanation
**Good (10–12):** Accurate but somewhat surface-level
**Developing (7–9):** Partial or uneven understanding
**Limited (0–6):** Confused, overly summary-driven, or inaccurate

---

### 2. Critical Analysis (20 pts)

* Evaluates strengths and limitations
* Engages thoughtfully with the author’s claims
* Moves beyond description into interpretation

**Excellent (17–20):** Sophisticated, nuanced critique
**Good (13–16):** Solid evaluation but somewhat predictable
**Developing (9–12):** Mostly summary with limited analysis
**Limited (0–8):** Little or no critical engagement

---

### 3. Connection to Course Themes (15 pts)

* Connects the book to at least two major course themes
* Demonstrates synthesis across weeks (history, representation, ethics, labor, technology, serious games, etc.)

**Excellent (13–15):** Strong, specific, well-integrated connections
**Good (10–12):** Clear but somewhat general connections
**Developing (7–9):** Minimal or underdeveloped connections
**Limited (0–6):** No meaningful connection to course material

---

### 4. Organization and Writing Quality (10 pts)

* Clear structure
* Logical progression
* Professional tone
* Minimal grammatical issues

---

## B. Video Review (20 points)

### 1. Clarity and Accuracy (8 pts)

* Explains the book clearly
* Represents ideas accurately
* Communicates major insights effectively

---

### 2. Engagement and Synthesis (7 pts)

* Highlights 2–3 meaningful insights
* Connects the book to broader gaming issues
* Feels intentional and practiced

---

### 3. Professional Effort (5 pts)

* Stays within time limit
* Organized and polished
* Clear audio/visual presentation

---

## C. Peer Review Participation (10 points)

Each student must:

* Watch 3 other group videos
* Submit thoughtful responses

Graded on:

* Completion (4 pts)
* Quality of insight (3 pts)
* Meaningful course connection (3 pts)

This is graded individually.

---

## D. Group Contribution (Peer-Scored) (10 points)

Each group member evaluates contribution levels. The goal is accountability without turning it into a popularity contest.

### Contribution Rating Scale (Confidential)

Each student privately rates every group member (including themselves) using this scale:

**5 – Exceptional Contributor**
Consistently prepared, took initiative, high-quality work, reliable leader.

**4 – Strong Contributor**
Dependable, completed tasks well, participated actively.

**3 – Adequate Contributor**
Completed assigned tasks but minimal initiative.

**2 – Limited Contributor**
Inconsistent participation or uneven effort.

**1 – Minimal Contributor**
Rarely participated or did not fulfill responsibilities.

Students must include:

* One sentence of justification per person
* Specific reference to behaviors (not personality)

Example:
“Completed the research section and helped revise the draft. Attended all meetings.”

### Group Average Calculation

1. Each student’s average peer score will be calculated.
2. Based on the average, contribution points will be assigned as follows:

| Average Score | Contribution Points (out of 10) |
| ------------- | ------------------------------- |
| 4.5 – 5.0     | 10 pts                          |
| 4.0 – 4.49    | 9 pts                           |
| 3.5 – 3.99    | 8 pts                           |
| 3.0 – 3.49    | 7 pts                           |
| 2.5 – 2.99    | 6 pts                           |
| Below 2.5     | Instructor review required      |

### Note on Fairness and Transparency

* Peer evaluations are confidential
* Scores are averaged
* The instructor reserves the right to override outliers
* Extreme ratings require justification
