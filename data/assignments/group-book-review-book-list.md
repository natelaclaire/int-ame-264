# Expanded Book Suggestion List

**INT/AME 264 – Group Book Review Project**

---

The following is an expanded list of book suggestions for the group book review project. Each title includes a brief description to help you choose a book that connects well to our course themes and supports your analysis. You can select from this list or propose your own book (must be approved in advance).

## Books About Specific Games

### *Masters of Doom* – David Kushner

Focus: Doom, Quake, id Software
A narrative history of John Carmack and John Romero and the creation of Doom and Quake. Explores early PC gaming culture, modding, technical innovation, and the rise of the FPS genre.

### *Console Wars* – Blake J. Harris

Focus: Sega vs. Nintendo
A detailed account of the 1990s console wars. Great for discussions of marketing, branding, globalization, and how corporate competition shaped gaming history.

### *The Perfect Game: Tetris – From Russia With Love* – Henk Rogers

Focus: Tetris
Tells the story of how Tetris moved from the Soviet Union to global dominance. Excellent for examining intellectual property, geopolitics, and early console licensing.

### *Significant Zero* – Walt Williams

Focus: Spec Ops: The Line
A memoir from a lead writer of Spec Ops: The Line. Strong choice for analyzing war narratives, ethics, and the tension between artistic ambition and industry constraints.

### *Blood, Sweat, and Pixels* – Jason Schreier

Focus: Multiple games (e.g., Diablo III, Halo Wars, Uncharted 4, Stardew Valley)
Behind-the-scenes accounts of how major games were made. Excellent for examining labor practices, crunch culture, and the realities of AAA and indie development.

### *Press Reset* – Jason Schreier

Focus: Studio closures and layoffs
Looks at what happens after successful games ship—layoffs, instability, and industry precarity. Strong for labor and ethics discussions.

### *Reality Is Broken* – Jane McGonigal

Focus: Game design philosophy
Argues that games can help solve real-world problems. Strong for connecting to serious games, civic engagement, and gamification.

## Books About Specific Franchises

### *The Legend of Zelda: Hyrule Historia*

Focus: Zelda franchise
An official history of the series, including timelines, development notes, and artwork. Good for worldbuilding analysis and franchise longevity.

### *Final Fantasy Ultimania Archive* (Volumes 1–3)

Focus: Final Fantasy series
Explores the evolution of one of the most influential RPG franchises. Useful for analyzing narrative structure, technological shifts, and Japanese game design.

### *The Making of Prince of Persia* – Jordan Mechner

Focus: Prince of Persia
Based on the creator’s journals. A fascinating look at early game development, rotoscoping, and indie creativity before “indie” was a category.

### *1001 Video Games You Must Play Before You Die* – Tony Mott (ed.)

Focus: Canon formation
Not about one game, but useful for examining how gaming history gets curated and who decides what “matters.”

### *Long Live Mortal Kombat: Round 1: The Fatalities and Fandom of the Arcade Era* – David L. Craddock

Focus: Mortal Kombat
A deep dive into the creation and cultural impact of Mortal Kombat. Strong for discussions of violence in games, censorship, and arcade culture.

---

## Books About Studios

### *Game Over* – David Sheff

Focus: Nintendo
Covers Nintendo’s rise and global expansion. Great for discussing corporate strategy, censorship policies, and the shaping of family-friendly gaming culture.

### *Not All Fairy Tales Have Happy Endings* – Ken Williams

Focus: Sierra On-Line
A firsthand account of Sierra’s rise and fall. Excellent for early PC history and the adventure game boom.

### *The Apple II Age* – Laine Nooney

Focus: Early personal computing
Examines how early home computers shaped gaming and digital culture. Strong for historical and cultural analysis.

### *The History of the Future* – Blake J. Harris

Focus: Oculus
Traces the development of virtual reality and the founding of Oculus. Strong for VR, tech optimism, and corporate acquisition discussions.

### *Stay Awhile and Listen* series (either book) – David L. Craddock

Focus: Blizzard North and Blizzard Entertainment
In-depth history of Blizzard’s two studios, their development teams, and their major franchises (Diablo, StarCraft, Warcraft). Excellent for examining studio culture, creative processes, and the evolution of PC gaming.

---

## Books About Developers / Game Creators

### *Sid Meier’s Memoir!* – Sid Meier

Focus: Civilization creator
A reflective look at design philosophy and innovation. Great for examining strategy games and simulation as educational tools.

### *Disrupting the Game* – Reggie Fils-Aimé

Focus: Nintendo leadership
Explores executive decision-making, corporate culture, and representation in leadership roles.

### *Ask Iwata* – Satoru Iwata

Focus: Nintendo’s late president
A collection of interviews and reflections. Excellent for discussions of design philosophy and leadership.

### *Doom Guy: Life in First Person* – John Romero

Focus: DOOM co-creator John Romero’s career
A memoir from one of the pioneers of the FPS genre. Strong for analyzing creativity, innovation, and the challenges of game development.

---

## Books About MMORPGs and Online Worlds

### *The Warcraft Diary* – John Staats

Focus: World of Warcraft
A behind-the-scenes look at the making of WoW. Strong for discussions of online communities, scale, and development complexity.

### *Play Between Worlds* – T.L. Taylor

Focus: EverQuest and MMOs
Explores player culture and social dynamics in online worlds. Excellent for community and identity analysis.

### *Watch Me Play* – T.L. Taylor

Focus: Twitch and streaming
Examines streaming culture and spectatorship. Strong for esports and creator economy discussions.

---

## Books About Esports

### *This Is Esports (and How to Spell It)* – Paul Chaloner

Focus: Competitive gaming
Traces the rise of esports. Connects well to discussions of multiplayer culture and commercialization.

### *Raising the Stakes* – T.L. Taylor

Focus: Professional gaming
Examines esports infrastructure, labor, and global competition.

---

## Books About Representation and Identity

### *Gaming at the Edge* – Adrienne Shaw

Focus: LGBTQ+ representation
Analyzes how identity and representation function in games and player communities.

### *Woke Gaming* – Edited by Kishonna L. Gray & David J. Leonard

Focus: Social justice and digital activism
Strong for connecting gaming to race, gender, and political identity.

### *Coin-Operated Americans* – Carly Kocurek

Focus: Arcade culture and masculinity
Examines how gaming became culturally coded as male.

### *Gaming Representation: Race, Gender, and Sexuality in Video Games* – Edited by Adrienne Shaw & Kristen Malkowski

Focus: Identity, power, and representation in games
A collection of essays examining how race, gender, and sexuality are portrayed in video games.

---

## Books About Design and Philosophy

### *The Art of Game Design* – Jesse Schell

Focus: Game design principles
More technical, but useful for analyzing how mechanics shape player experience.

### *Rules of Play* – Salen & Zimmerman

Focus: Game design theory
Foundational text on how games function structurally and culturally.

### *A Theory of Fun for Game Design* – Raph Koster

Focus: What makes games engaging
Short, accessible, and good for design-focused analysis.

### *The Psychology of Video Games* – Celia Hodent

Focus: Cognitive science, player experience, and game design
Explores how psychological principles can inform better game design and player engagement.

---

## Books About Gaming Culture and History

### *Replay* – Tristan Donovan

Focus: Global gaming history
A comprehensive history of gaming across cultures and decades. Strong for connecting to course themes of globalization and cultural impact.

### *The Ultimate History of Video Games* – Steven Kent

Focus: Industry history and behind-the-scenes development
Covers the industry from its origins to the present. Useful for broad historical context and understanding industry trends.

### *The Game Console 2.0: A Photographic History from Atari to Xbox* – Evan Amos

Focus: Console design and history
A visual history of game consoles, exploring design evolution and cultural significance.

### *Hackers: Heroes of the Computer Revolution* – Steven Levy

Focus: Early computing and hacker culture
While not exclusively about gaming, it provides important context for the early days of computer culture and the origins of game development, particularly in part 3 of the book.
